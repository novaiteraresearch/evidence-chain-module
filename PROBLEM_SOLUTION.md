# Problem & Solution

## Problem

High-stakes organizations (defense, government, research labs, and AI companies) need AI systems that will not catastrophically fail, leak, or misbehave under adversarial pressure. Most AI work they encounter is model-centric or demo-centric, not architecture- and governance-centric.

They also struggle to evaluate individual practitioners claiming "AI safety" expertise because portfolios resemble generic development resumes rather than evidence-backed safety architecture and operational rigor.

## Solution

Nova Itera offers an AI safety architecture and governance practice that treats AI systems like defense infrastructure: adversarially tested, provenance-tracked, cryptographically grounded, and governed by explicit safety and auditability protocols.

Concretely, the solution is a research-backed capability stack comprising:

- **ATQ** (Adversarial Threat Qualification)
- **CTIR** (Cryptographic Tamper-evident Integrity Records)
- **Probabilistic prompt validation**
- **Metaprompt specification**
- **Provenance-aware framework**

This stack is complemented by applied experience in defense contexts.
